Plato (/ˈpleɪtoʊ/ PLAY-toe;[1] Greek: Πλάτων Plátōn; 428/427 or 424/423 – 348/347 BC) was an
ancient Greek philosopher born in Athens during the Classical period in Ancient Greece. In Athens,
Plato founded the Academy, a philosophical school where he taught the philosophical doctrines
that would later became known as Platonism. Plato (or Platon) was a pen name derived from his
nickname
